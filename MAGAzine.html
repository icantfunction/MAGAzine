OUR NEW CURRICULUM IS HERE

Our effort to restructure our curriculum with a more project-based focus

By David Ramos

First, let's talk fashion. Those Vice City jerseys were cool for a minute, but let's be real: it looks like they raided a neon graveyard. Every game feels like a throwback to a 1980s Miami nightclub, and not in a good way. Miami Heat: where basketball meets bad taste in retro attire.

Now onto their playing style. If you enjoy the thrilling sight of three-point attempts clanging off the rim, then Miami Heat games are your go-to entertainment. It's like they think the hoop is moving. Spoiler: it's not. Maybe they should check their GPS settings.

Leadership? Oh, you mean the spinning carousel that is their team strategy? One day it's all about defense, the next it's... well, who knows? It's like following a plot in a telenovela. Just when you think you've got it figured out, another twist!

“The best way to learn is to do; the worst way to teach is to talk.”

Speaking of drama, the Heat could give soap operas a run for their money. Between the on-court theatrics and the sideline soapbox speeches, you could switch the channel from sports to daytime TV and hardly notice the difference.

Player development? Sure, if by development you mean passing the ball around the perimeter and hoping someone eventually takes a shot. It's not so much a game plan as it is a game of hot potato. “You shoot!” “No, you shoot!” Guys, please, someone just shoot.

Let's not forget the fan base. Heat fans are known for arriving fashionably late and leaving... well, early. They say it's Miami time, but really, it's more like, “We'll show up if they're winning.”

And finally, the team motto. “Heat Nation” sounds less like a fan base and more like a weather report. Maybe it's a warning about the climate change they bring with their icy cold shooting streaks?
